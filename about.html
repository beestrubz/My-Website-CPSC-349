About me

Hey there, thanks for stopping by! I’m Brandon, a student at Cal State Fullerton finishing up my Bachelor of Science in Computer Science. I’m someone who loves artistic expression—whether it’s playing the guitar, dabbling with keys, or watching animated films. I also, am an avid lover of the outdoors as it inspires me. However, I’m equally fascinated by the nerdy technical stuff. I’m always asking myself, “How does that thing work the way it does?”. I am a lifelong learner and I love learning to skills. That curiosity led me to study Computer Science, where I would get the opportunity to build cool software.

But it was not just any software that excited me—no, it was video games. I love playing video games, and I also love to watch documentaries and read about how games were made. Simply put: I love games, so I want to make them! But I’ve always wondered—how can I combine my love for visual arts, like animation, with the technical magic of programming? That question led me to the role of a Technical Artist, someone who bridges the gap between art and technology. A Technical Artist ensures that creative visions come to life by optimizing workflows, solving technical challenges, and blending artistic creativity with programming expertise.

Traditional schooling taught me the theory: coding, algorithms, and all that good computer science stuff. But making video games? That’s something I’ve been teaching myself in my free time. I began creating 2D-pixel art games in 2019 using the Unity game engine, learning through YouTube tutorials and online forums. Fast forward to today, I’ve transitioned to developing immersive 3D experiences, crafting assets in Blender, and bringing them to life with Unreal Engine.
